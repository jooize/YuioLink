Two-Type Namespace — UI Mockups

The namespace is now just two shapes, and the choice between them is a privacy choice — so the UI should teach that, not bury it under "view limit." Below: the shipped picker, a stronger reframe, a future dial, and new features the entropy work unlocks.

Unlimited → 1 wordyuio.link/mango — short & handy, ~12 bits, fully guessable. Convenient, not private.
One-time → 4 wordsyuio.link/runnyDUSKoutletDELUGE — ~47 bits, unguessable. The name itself guards the single view.
The name got longOne-time URLs are 18–28 chars. Copy, QR, and autocomplete matter more now.

    Recommendations

    1. Ship A or B. Both make the privacy tradeoff legible. A (cards) teaches best for newcomers; B (segmented + live preview) keeps the form compact and is the smaller change from what's shipped. I lean B for the default, with A's copy folded into its verdict line.
    2. Show the name shape before creating. This is the highest-value new idea: the user sees mango vs runnyDUSKoutletDELUGE and instantly understands why one is private and one isn't. It turns the entropy math into a felt UI fact.
    3. Lead with Copy + add QR on the result. Long one-time names make manual entry painful; copy and QR remove that friction.
    4. Autocomplete the "open a link" field (D). Dictionary words make this cheap and it directly offsets the longer names. Defer if scope-bound.
    5. Keep the dial (C) in our back pocket. Two types is the right v1; the third "Unlisted" rung is a clean future extension on the same control if a "casually private but reusable" need appears.
    6. Drop "Limit views to" framing. It described a counter; the real choice is reusable-vs-one-time / public-vs-private. Rename accordingly.
