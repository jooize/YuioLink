Expires After — slider mockups

All four are live — drag them. Each shows a big readout of what is set, fine steps in the minutes range, coarser through hours and days.

A · Stepped stops

The slider moves through a fixed ladder of sensible durations (1 m … 7 d). Every position is a value a human would pick — nothing like "43 minutes" can happen. Most predictable; least precise.

B · Logarithmic with magnetic detents

Continuous log scale from 1 minute to 7 days — half the track is under an hour, so the low range is roomy. The thumb snaps to the old presets (10 m, 1 h, 1 d, 7 d) when it lands near one; between detents it rounds to friendly steps (1 m under an hour, 15 m under a day, 1 h beyond).

C · Band picker + fine slider

Coarse choice first (Minutes / Hours / Days), then one slider whose whole width covers just that band — so minutes get 1-minute precision, hours 1-hour, days 1-day. Two controls, but each is dead simple, and it maps 1:1 onto the existing Specify field.

D · Log slider + tappable exact value

Same log track as B, but the readout is a button: tap it and it becomes a number-plus-unit field for an exact value (the current Specify path, demoted to an escape hatch). The slider covers 99% of picks; typing covers the rest.
